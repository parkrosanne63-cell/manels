About the Giant Panda

Nature's Most
Beloved Bear

The giant panda is a bear native to South Central China. Despite their bear classification, they are renowned for their gentle, solitary nature and their near-exclusive bamboo diet.

Recognized by their distinctive black-and-white coat, giant pandas have become one of the world's most iconic animals and a global symbol for wildlife conservation.

Behavior & Biology

Life of a Panda

🎋

Bamboo Devotion

Pandas spend 10–16 hours per day eating, consuming 12–38 kg of bamboo daily. Their pseudo-thumb helps them grasp stalks.

🐣

Tiny Cubs

Newborn panda cubs weigh only 100–200g — about 1/900th of their mother's size, the smallest relative to mother mammals.

🏔️

Mountain Climbers

Wild pandas live in mountainous forests at high elevations. They are strong swimmers and excellent climbers.

🌙

Solitary Creatures

Giant pandas are largely solitary animals, each establishing a home range in the mountains. They communicate via scent marks, scratches on trees, and vocalizations to avoid direct encounters.

🎵

Mountain Climbers

Pandas communicate through a repertoire of about 11 distinct vocalizations, including bleating, honking, and a unique "moan" used during mating season.

💤

Mountain Climbers

Unlike other bears, giant pandas do not hibernate. Their low-nutrition bamboo diet means they must eat year-round and rest frequently between long eating sessions.

WHERE THEY LIVE

The Misty
Bamboo Forests

Giant pandas live in the mountain ranges of central China — primarily in Sichuan province, with smaller populations in Shaanxi and Gansu. These cool, wet bamboo forests provide everything a panda needs.

CONSERVATION STATUS

A Story of
Hope & Recovery

In 2016, the giant panda was downlisted from "Endangered" to "Vulnerable" on the IUCN Red List — a landmark conservation success fueled by decades of dedicated protection, habitat restoration, and captive breeding.

🛡️
67 Reserves

Protected Reserves

China has established 67 panda nature reserves, protecting 66% of the wild panda population and 53% of their habitat.

🌱
+11.8% Habitat

Habitat Restoration

Reforestation efforts have connected fragmented habitats, creating wildlife corridors that allow panda populations to mix and thrive.

🐼
600+ in Captivity

Captive Breeding

Over 600 pandas now live in captivity across China and abroad. Successful captive breeding programs have contributed to reintroduction efforts.
